Home of the National Library of Ireland

The National Library of Ireland is a key institution dedicated to collecting, cataloging, and preserving records that chronicle the nation's history, governance, and cultural heritage. It operates under the jurisdiction of the National Archives of Ireland while maintaining its operational autonomy. The Library’s core mission is to safeguard documents related to civil service appointments, government statements, and other vital historical data that contribute to understanding the functioning of the state.

About the Library

The Library plays a crucial role in safeguarding official records. It's objectives include:

  • Collecting, cataloging, and preserving civil service appointments, government and public agency official statements, and other significant historical data related to the functioning of the state.
  • Establishing and maintaining an organized archive system that ensures easy access to historical data for research, transparency, and accountability purposes.
  • Collaborating with relevant government bodies and agencies to ensure the regular transfer of official records and documents to the Library's custody.

As a repository of government documents, the The Library has the following functions:

  • Acquiring, cataloging, and preserving documents, records, and other forms of information related to civil service appointments, government activities, and public agency operations.
  • Digitizing and making accessible a significant portion of its collection to the public through digital platforms, while ensuring the protection of sensitive information.
  • Providing expert advice and assistance to the National Archives, government bodies, researchers, and the public on matters related to official records and historical data.

Key Personnel

Director General of the National Archives

Incumbent: Ms. Ciara C. Heathcote

The Director General of the National Archives oversees the management and strategic direction of the archives. They ensure that the institution's mission is carried out efficiently and effectively, promoting the preservation of national records and overseeing key projects related to archiving and document management. Ms. Heathcote is the only female to take the post of Director General of the National Archives in Ireland's history and formally served as Tánaiste na hÉireann.

National Librarian

Incumbent: Dr. Dave O'Cullane PhD

The National Librarian (officially styled as the Director-General of the National Library) is responsible for overseeing the collection, cataloging, and preservation of documents, records, and other significant historical data related to the functioning of the state. They ensure that the Library’s resources are organized and accessible for research, transparency, and accountability purposes, and they play a vital role in collaborating with government bodies to facilitate the regular transfer of official records and documents to the Library’s custody.

The Current Government

The current government is committed to maintaining transparency, accountability, and preserving the heritage of the nation. Under the leadership of the Taoiseach and the Minister for Communities, Culture and Language, the government works closely with the Library to ensure that important documents are properly stored and protected for public access and future generations.
